The City of Los Angeles accounts for 57.6% of all 48,085 inspections in this dataset, with 27,686 inspections across the study period. The next largest jurisdictions are Glendale (5.9%), Burbank (4.6%), and Santa Monica (2.5%). No other city exceeds 2% of total inspections. The City of Los Angeles is one of 85 cities under contract with LA County's Environmental Health Division, yet accounts for more than half of all inspections. That level of concentration in a single jurisdiction shapes how countywide trends should be interpreted.

What This Suggests

82% is the countywide average, but no city actually experienced an 82% decline. Burbank lost 10% of its inspections. Paramount lost all of them, from 115 down to zero. The county-level number hides two entirely different realities.

Because DIB has no required inspection schedule for incorporated cities, each city's inspection count reflects choices about where DIB sent inspectors, not necessarily conditions on the ground.

Same nine months, radically different directions
Burbank
Fell only 10%, from 662 inspections in January through September 2023 to 598 in the same months of 2025. One contracted city kept nearly its full inspection volume while its neighbors lost almost everything.
Glendale
Fell 99%, from 1,858 inspections to 20.
Paramount
115 inspections in January through September 2023. Zero in the same months of 2025.
Sierra Madre
40 inspections in January through September 2023. Zero in the same months of 2025.

Twelve cities in total had inspection activity in 2023 and none at all in January through September 2025. The two largest inspection cities moved with the countywide trend, Los Angeles down 83% (15,572 to 2,670) and Santa Monica down 94% (912 to 59). Nothing in the data explains why coverage continued in one city and stopped in another.

This page suppresses violation rates for areas under 10 inspections because a rate is statistically meaningless at that size. But the small count is itself the finding. Sixteen of the 95 cities and areas in this analysis fall under that threshold across the entire period. A city showing four inspections is not necessarily a healthy city. It may simply be an uninspected one.
