Public Health in Housing: An Analysis of LA County Inspection Trends

An analysis of 48,085 housing inspections and 37,026 violations from the LA County Department of Public Health, covering January 2023 through September 2025.

What the Data Shows
Demographic Analysis
Across 254 ZIP codes, income, housing costs, and racial and ethnic composition did not explain where inspections fell. The decline was systemic, not concentrated by income, housing cost, or race across ZIP codes.
No Inspection Schedule Required
No law requires county public health inspectors to inspect any city building on any schedule. Other county housing programs operate on mandated inspection cycles. This one does not, even though LA has the highest rentership rate of any major U.S. metro, 53% of households rent, and renters have no direct way to require an inspection the way an owner controls their own property.
Overlapping Authority
In the City of LA, the District Inspection Branch and the city's housing program inspect the same buildings, one for public health hazards and one for housing code violations. When District Inspection Branch inspections decline, the city program does not fill that gap.
